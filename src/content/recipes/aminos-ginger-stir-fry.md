---
title: "Classic Aminos & Ginger Stir-Fry"
description: "A sweet-savoury coconut aminos glaze over seared protein and crisp vegetables. Scaled for four, metric measurements throughout."
category: "Dinner"
serves: "4"
prepTime: "15 min"
cookTime: "10 min"
tags: ["Weeknight", "One pan", "Gluten free"]
favourite: true
ingredients:
  - "160 ml coconut aminos — the sweet-savoury base"
  - "120 ml water or stock (vegetable or chicken)"
  - "30 ml rice vinegar — about 2 tbsp, for acidity balance"
  - "30 ml toasted sesame oil — about 2 tbsp, for rich nutty depth"
  - "18 g garlic — about 6 cloves, finely minced"
  - "3 g ground ginger — about 1 tsp, whisked early to prevent clumping"
  - "8 g arrowroot powder (or cornstarch) — essential for thickening the glaze"
  - "Protein of choice — chicken, beef, or tofu"
  - "Mixed vegetables — broccoli, carrots, spring onions, snap peas"
  - "High-heat oil, for frying"
---

1. **Prep & whisk the sauce** — In a jug, combine the arrowroot powder and ground ginger. Whisk in the cold water or stock until entirely clump-free. Add the coconut aminos, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and minced garlic. Stir thoroughly and set aside right next to the hob.
2. **Sear the protein (batch 1)** — Heat 1 tbsp high-heat oil in a large wok or wide pan until shimmering. Add your prepared protein in a single layer. Let it sear untouched for 1–2 minutes to get a crisp crust, toss for another 2 minutes until cooked, then transfer to a clean plate. Do not clean the pan.
3. **Stir-fry the vegetables (batch 2)** — Add another splash of high-heat oil to the hot pan if dry. Fry dense vegetables (broccoli, carrots) first for 2–3 minutes until vibrant. Add delicate vegetables (spring onions, snap peas) and toss for another 1–2 minutes until tender-crisp.
4. **Combine and glaze** — Return the protein and its juices to the wok. Give the sauce jug one final, thorough whisk to lift any settled starch, then pour it straight into the middle of the roaring hot pan. Toss everything constantly for 60–90 seconds until a glossy, clear glaze tightly coats the ingredients. Serve immediately.

> **Pro-tip:** Cooking a stir-fry for four means high volume. Staggering the protein and vegetables in batches prevents the pan from losing heat and steaming the ingredients instead of crisping them.
