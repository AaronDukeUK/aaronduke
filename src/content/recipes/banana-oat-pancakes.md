title: "Banana Oat Blender Pancakes"
description: "Three-ingredient pancakes whizzed up in the blender — oats, banana, and plant milk. Soft enough for little hands, good enough for everyone else's breakfast."
category: "Toddler Snacks"
serves: "Makes 6–7"
prepTime: "5 min"
cookTime: "20 min"
tags: ["Dairy free", "Egg free", "Vegan", "Blender", "Freezes well"]
source: "https://vancouverwithlove.com/3-ingredient-vegan-pancakes/"
ingredients:
  - "180 g rolled oats"
  - "2 ripe bananas"
  - "540 ml plant milk — oat, almond, or soy"
  - "A little oil, for the pan (optional with a good non-stick)"
---

1. **Blitz the oats** — Add the oats to a blender and blend on high for 20 seconds until they turn to flour.
2. **Make the batter** — Loosen any flour stuck to the sides, add the bananas and plant milk, and blend for 30 seconds until smooth. Don't let the batter sit longer than 10 minutes before cooking — the oats keep thickening it.
3. **Heat the pan** — Warm 1 teaspoon of oil in a frying pan over medium heat until a drop of water sizzles on contact.
4. **Cook** — Pour in a small ladle of batter per pancake, tilting the pan to help it spread. Cook for 2–3 minutes until bubble holes form and the top looks set, then flip and cook for about 2 minutes more until golden. Repeat, adding a little oil between batches.

> **Pro-tip:** Medium heat is the trick — too hot and the outside burns before the middle cooks. Keeps 3 days in the fridge and freezes well; thaw fully before serving. Cut into strips for smaller toddlers.
