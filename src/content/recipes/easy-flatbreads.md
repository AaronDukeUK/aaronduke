---
title: "Easy Pan Flatbreads"
description: "Soft, puffy flatbreads with no yeast, no yogurt, and no dairy — just flour, oil, and water, cooked in a hot pan in minutes."
category: "Basics"
serves: "Makes 8"
prepTime: "25 min"
cookTime: "3 min each"
tags: ["Dairy free", "Egg free", "Vegan", "No yeast"]
source: "https://domesticgothess.com/blog/2020/07/14/easy-flatbreads/"
ingredients:
  - "500 g white bread flour (plain flour works too), plus extra for rolling"
  - "1 tsp baking powder"
  - "1 tsp fine salt"
  - "60 ml olive oil (sunflower or vegetable oil also fine)"
  - "300 ml lukewarm water — you may not need it all"
  - "A little extra oil, for the pan"
---

1. **Make the dough** — Combine the flour, baking powder, and salt in a large bowl. Add the oil and most of the water, mixing gradually until it comes together into a soft dough — hold back the last splash of water and add only if needed.
2. **Knead and rest** — Knead on an unfloured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Cover with a tea towel and rest for 15 minutes.
3. **Divide and roll** — Split the dough into 8 equal pieces and shape into balls. On a floured surface, roll one ball into a thin round, roughly 20–22 cm.
4. **Cook** — Heat a heavy-bottomed frying pan over medium-high heat with a light coating of oil. Cook the flatbread for 1–2 minutes per side until browned in spots — it should puff up. Press gently with a spatula if it balloons. Repeat with the rest, re-oiling the pan lightly as you go.

> **Pro-tip:** Pan temperature is everything — too low and they turn dry and crackery, too high and they burn before cooking through. Cooled flatbreads keep 2–3 days in a sealed bag and freeze for up to 3 months; reheat straight from frozen under the grill.
