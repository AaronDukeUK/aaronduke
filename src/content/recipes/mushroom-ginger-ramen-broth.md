---
title: "Savoury Mushroom & Ginger Ramen Broth"
description: "A deep-umami ramen broth built on dried shiitake, bruised ginger, and caramelised soy and mirin. Strain, pour over fresh noodles, and top with the sliced mushrooms."
category: "Basics"
serves: "2–3"
prepTime: "5 min"
cookTime: "35 min"
tags: ["Umami", "Vegan", "Pairs with noodles"]
ingredients:
  - "20 g dried shiitake mushrooms — crucial for deep, foundational umami"
  - "25 g fresh ginger — sliced into thick coins and lightly bruised"
  - "15 g garlic — about 4 cloves, crushed flat"
  - "45 ml soy sauce — about 3 tbsp, for savoury salt depth"
  - "30 ml mirin — about 2 tbsp, for subtle sweetness"
  - "15 ml rice vinegar — about 1 tbsp, to cut through the richness"
  - "15 ml toasted sesame oil — for the aromatics base"
  - "1 litre water"
---

1. **Extract the aromatics** — Heat the toasted sesame oil in a medium pot over medium heat. Add the bruised ginger coins and crushed garlic cloves. Sauté for 2 minutes until incredibly fragrant and lightly golden on the edges, making sure they do not burn.
2. **Deglaze and add liquid** — Pour in the mirin and soy sauce, letting them bubble for 30 seconds to caramelise slightly. Pour in the water and drop the dried shiitake mushrooms directly into the liquid.
3. **Simmer for depth** — Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer gently for 25–30 minutes. This slowly rehydrates the mushrooms and extracts their deep glutamate profile into the broth.
4. **Finish and strain** — Remove from the heat. Stir in the rice vinegar to brighten the top notes. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean jug, reserving the rehydrated shiitakes to slice thin as a noodle topping. Pour piping hot over fresh ramen noodles.

> **Pro-tip:** To elevate the body of this broth further, whisk in a single teaspoon of white miso paste just before serving. Never boil the broth after adding miso, or you will mute its delicate flavour.
