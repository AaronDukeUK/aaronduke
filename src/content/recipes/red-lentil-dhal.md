---
title: "Red Lentil & Coconut Dhal"
description: "A silky one-pot dhal of red lentils simmered in coconut milk with warming spices, finished with lemon. Naturally vegan and gluten free."
category: "Dinner"
serves: "4"
prepTime: "5 min"
cookTime: "25 min"
tags: ["Vegan", "One pot", "Gluten free", "Freezes well"]
source: "https://veganonboard.com/red-lentil-dahl-with-coconut-milk/"
ingredients:
  - "200 g split red lentils"
  - "1 tsp coconut oil"
  - "1 onion, chopped"
  - "3 garlic cloves, minced"
  - "1 tbsp fresh ginger, grated"
  - "1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped"
  - "1 tsp ground cumin"
  - "1 tsp ground coriander"
  - "1 tsp ground turmeric"
  - "¼ tsp ground cinnamon"
  - "½ tsp fine salt"
  - "400 ml can coconut milk (full fat for best results)"
  - "360 ml water"
  - "1 tbsp lemon juice"
  - "To serve: rice or flatbreads, coconut cream, fresh coriander"
---

1. **Rinse the lentils** — Rinse the red lentils in cold water until it runs mostly clear, then drain.
2. **Soften the onion** — Heat the coconut oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and cook for about 5 minutes until soft and translucent.
3. **Add the aromatics** — Add the garlic, ginger, and chilli and sauté for one minute until fragrant.
4. **Bloom the spices** — Stir in the cumin, coriander, turmeric, cinnamon, and salt and cook for 30 seconds to release the flavours.
5. **Simmer** — Add the lentils, coconut milk, and water. Bring to the boil, then reduce the heat, cover, and simmer for about 20 minutes until the lentils are tender, stirring occasionally so nothing catches.
6. **Finish and serve** — Take off the heat and stir in the lemon juice. Serve over rice or with the pan flatbreads, topped with a swirl of coconut cream and fresh coriander.

> **Pro-tip:** The dhal thickens as it cools — loosen leftovers with a splash of water when reheating. It freezes well for up to 3 months.
