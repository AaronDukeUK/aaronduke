---
title: "Ultra-Creamy Hummus"
description: "Whipped, silky-smooth hummus built on an emulsified tahini base — the ice water drizzle at the end is the secret weapon."
category: "Dips & Sides"
serves: "4–6"
prepTime: "10 min"
tags: ["Blender", "Vegan", "Make ahead"]
ingredients:
  - "1 can (400 g) chickpeas — drained weight approx. 240 g"
  - "60 ml tahini — about ¼ cup, for authentic nutty richness"
  - "60 ml fresh lemon juice — about ¼ cup, for a bright, clean zip"
  - "1 small garlic clove — kept minimal so it doesn't overpower"
  - "30 ml extra virgin olive oil — about 2 tbsp, plus extra for serving"
  - "2 g ground cumin — about ½ tsp, for earthy warmth"
  - "3 g fine salt — about ½ tsp, to lift the flavours"
  - "30–45 ml ice water — 2 to 3 tbsp, to whip it fluffy"
  - "Paprika, to serve"
---

1. **Emulsify the base** — Add the lemon juice, tahini, and salt to your food processor or high-powered blender. Blend for a full 1–2 minutes until the mixture transforms into a thick, pale, creamy paste.
2. **Add aromatics** — Add the minced garlic, ground cumin, and olive oil to the base. Blend for another 30 seconds, scraping down the sides of the bowl to ensure full incorporation.
3. **Blend chickpeas** — Drain the chickpeas completely. Add half of them to the processor and blend for 1 minute. Add the remaining half and blend for another 1–2 minutes until completely smooth. The mixture will be quite thick at this point.
4. **The ice water whip** — While the motor is running, slowly drizzle in the ice water 1 tablespoon at a time. Watch the texture shift: it will instantly lighten in colour and transform into an incredibly light, fluffy cloud. Spread into deep swirls on a plate and top with olive oil and paprika.

> **Pro-tip:** If you have an extra 5 minutes, boil the drained chickpeas in a small pan of water with a pinch of baking soda until mushy. Blending them while warm yields an incredibly silky, restaurant-style finish.
