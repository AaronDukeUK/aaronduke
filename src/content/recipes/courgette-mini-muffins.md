---
title: "Courgette Mini Muffins"
description: "Moist little veg-packed muffins made in the food processor — no egg, no dairy, and no squeezing the courgette. Toddler-sized and freezer friendly."
category: "Toddler Snacks"
serves: "Makes 24 mini"
prepTime: "10 min"
cookTime: "12–14 min"
tags: ["Dairy free", "Egg free", "Vegan", "Hidden veg", "Freezes well"]
source: "https://www.yummytoddlerfood.com/vegan-zucchini-mini-muffins/"
ingredients:
  - "250 g courgette, roughly chopped (about 1 medium)"
  - "45 g rolled oats"
  - "1 tsp ground cinnamon"
  - "1 tsp baking powder"
  - "½ tsp bicarbonate of soda"
  - "¼ tsp fine salt"
  - "120 g unsweetened apple purée"
  - "60 ml melted coconut oil (or a neutral vegetable oil)"
  - "60 ml maple syrup"
  - "1 tsp vanilla extract"
  - "180 g wholemeal flour"
  - "60 g mini chocolate chips (optional, dairy free)"
---

1. **Prep** — Heat the oven to 190°C and grease a 24-hole mini muffin tin.
2. **Grate the courgette** — Blitz the courgette in a food processor for about 30 seconds until finely grated. No need to squeeze out the moisture — it's what keeps the muffins soft.
3. **Mix the wet** — Add the oats, cinnamon, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, salt, apple purée, coconut oil, maple syrup, and vanilla. Process for another 30 seconds.
4. **Fold in the flour** — Add the flour (and chocolate chips, if using) and pulse until just combined — the batter will be thick.
5. **Bake** — Spoon a heaped tablespoon of batter into each hole and bake for 12–14 minutes, until a skewer comes out clean.
6. **Cool** — Rest in the tin for 5 minutes, then move to a wire rack.

> **Pro-tip:** Keeps 3 days in the fridge or freezes for up to 3 months. Dice or halve for smaller babies. Swap in a gluten-free flour blend and certified GF oats to make them gluten free too.
